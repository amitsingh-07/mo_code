Eligibility
Who us eligible to write a will?
Any one who is at least age 21 and above and of sound mind can write a will.

About Will Writing
What are the benefits of writing a will?
  • You decide who and how your estate should be distributed instead of leaving to the law to decide.
  • Avoid dispute among family members
  • Expedite settlement of estate with a will than one without it.
  • Appointment of Guardian – to care for the minor children.
  • Appointment of Executor or Trustee – This is the person who will be dealing on the estate and ensure the wishes in the will is carried out.
What happens to the estate if one dies without a will?
When one dies without a will, an administrator must step forward to apply for the Letter of Administration before he/she can distribute the estate according to the Intestate Succession Act. If there are young children involved, 2 administrators as well as 2 guarantors are needed before the court would grant the letter.

What assets cannot be distributed via a will?
  • CPF nomination – Your CPF balances will be distributed according to your CPF Nomination if you have made one. Your will cannot supersede your CPF nomination.
  • Insurance policies under Trust Nomination (S73A or S49L)
  • Property under Joint Tenancy – the property will be passed over to the survivor joint tenant.
  • Savings under Joint Account – the savings will be passed over to the survivor joint tenant.
When should you revise your will?
You should review your will from time to time to ensure that it continue to serve your distribution wishes. These are situations where you should review and amend your will if necessary:
  • Change in distribution wishes
  • Change or death of any of beneficiaries, guardian, executor, trustee
  • Marriage or re-marriage – these events will revoke your earlier will you have made.
  • Change in personal circumstances such as addition of new family member
When will your current will be revoked?
  • Upon marriage or re-marriage
  • When a later will is written
What should you do besides having Will?
  • CPF nomination – you can read more from CPF website and download the form here.
  • Schedule of assets – this is a list of assets that you have, eg, property, bank accounts, investment, overseas assets.
  • You should keep your physical will in a safe place and informed your loved ones where it is kept. One good place is to keep it together with your insurance policies.
What if I have a bank account or other assets in other countries?
Consult a lawyer because your will may not be enforceable under different jurisdiction.
